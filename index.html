What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack that masquerades as legitimate communication to steal sensitive information.

  • Cyber attack masquerading as legitimate communication
  • Goal: Steal sensitive information
  • Uses social engineering tactics
  • Can target individuals or organizations

The Cost of Phishing

  • 83% of organizations experienced phishing attacks in 2023
  • Average cost of a data breach: $4.35 million
  • 95% of cybersecurity breaches due to human error
  • Billions lost annually to phishing scams

Types of Phishing Attacks

  1. Email Phishing
  2. Spear Phishing
  3. Whaling
  4. Smishing (SMS Phishing)
  5. Vishing (Voice Phishing)
  6. Clone Phishing
  7. Website Spoofing

Email Phishing Deep Dive

Common characteristics:

  • Urgency
  • Generic greetings
  • Poor grammar
  • Suspicious sender addresses
  • Unusual requests

Best Practices

  • Verify Sender
    • Check email address carefully
    • Cross-check with known contacts
  • Hover Over Links
    • Reveal full URL before clicking
  • Do Not Share Sensitive Info
    • Avoid sharing personal details via email or message
  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication
    • Enhance account security
  • Report Suspicious Emails
    • Inform IT/security team immediately

Interactive Scenario #1

Imagine you receive this email:

              
                From: it-support@company.com
                Subject: Important: Password Update Required
                Date: [Current Date]

                Dear [Your Name],

                Our IT team has detected a security issue and is asking all
                employees to update their passwords immediately to prevent
                unauthorized access.

                Please click the link below to update your password now:

                [Update Password](http://suspiciouslink.com)

                Thank you for your cooperation.

                Best regards,
                IT Support Team

What should you do?

  • Hover over the link to check the URL
  • Verify sender address
  • Contact IT directly using known contact info
  • Do not click the link

Interactive Scenario #2

You receive a text message:

              
                From: [Local Bank]

                [Security Alert]:
                Your account has been temporarily locked due to suspicious activity.
                Please click the link below to unlock your account:

                [http://secure-bank-login.com]

What should you do?

  • Do not click the link
  • Verify message with your bank
  • Check your account via official banking app/website
  • Report the message to your bank

Reporting Phishing Attacks

  1. Do not click any links or download attachments
  2. Forward the email/message to your IT/security team
  3. Include headers if forwarding an email
  4. Delete the suspicious email/message
  5. Run a virus scan if you've interacted with the email
